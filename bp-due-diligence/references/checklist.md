# BP Due Diligence Checklist

Use this as a coverage checklist, not as a rigid report format.

## Founder and Team

- Full legal names, aliases, Chinese/English spelling variants.
- Education: official university pages, thesis records, alumni/news, degree dates.
- Employment: official company pages, press releases, patents, papers, GitHub, conference bios.
- Role accuracy: employee, advisor, contractor, professor, PI, board member, consultant.
- Time commitment: full-time or part-time, cross-border working pattern, governance.
- Relevance: whether prior work is directly relevant to the BP product, not just prestigious.

## Company and Ownership

- Legal entity name, registration number, formation date, registered capital, address, legal representative.
- Shareholders, ultimate beneficial owners, option pools, ESOP, nominee/holding partnerships.
- Latest official registry documents where possible. Treat public aggregators as secondary.
- Related entities in other jurisdictions, intercompany IP/license arrangements.
- Funding claims, previous rounds, SAFE/convertible notes, debt, grants, government subsidies.

## Papers, Awards, and Technical Provenance

- Paper titles, venues, years, author positions, citation counts, code links, awards.
- Venue quality: top conference/journal, workshop, arXiv/preprint, poster, demo/system track.
- Project relevance: direct algorithm/system behind the BP, adjacent work, or only reputation.
- Reproducibility: code, datasets, benchmarks, third-party replications.
- IP chain: university ownership, open-source license, patent assignment, commercialization rights.

## Product Verification

- Public URLs, docs, pricing, app downloads, APIs, demos, trial registration.
- Hands-on test path: registration, verification, install, first workflow, expected output.
- App trust: signing/notarization, binary version, update channel, network endpoints, logs.
- Failure modes: auth errors, download failures, local model availability, billing restrictions.
- Data/privacy/security: local vs cloud execution, token handling, logs, local ports, data retention.

## Open Source and Ecosystem

- GitHub: stars, forks, commits, contributors, releases, issues, PRs, license, commit recency.
- Hugging Face/model hubs: model count, downloads, likes, followers, last update, model cards.
- Package registries: downloads, versions, dependency health.
- Adoption quality: external contributors, customer references, integrations, citations.
- Compare against incumbents and substitutes, not only exact-match projects.

## Market, Competitors, and Customer Evidence

- Direct competitors, open-source alternatives, cloud incumbents, internal-build substitutes.
- What the BP claims as unique vs what existing tools already provide.
- Public customer proof: named case studies, app reviews, forums, GitHub issues, social posts.
- Pricing and willingness to pay: free alternatives, enterprise pain, compliance drivers.
- GTM realism: buyer persona, sales cycle, deployment burden, support needs.

## Red Flags

- Prestigious advisors with unclear role or time commitment.
- Academic project presented as company-owned commercial IP without authorization proof.
- “Hugging Face for X”, “AI OS”, “offline AI”, “agent platform” without ecosystem mechanics.
- Claims that existing LLMs/agent frameworks already satisfy, with no benchmark delta.
- Product demo unavailable, gated by private account, or fails basic workflow.
- Cross-border team operating in regulated data contexts without compliance plan.
- Security-sensitive product logs tokens or exposes unauthenticated local services.

## Public-Depth Boundary

Explicitly state what cannot be verified from public sources, such as:

- Latest official shareholder list or beneficial ownership.
- Private contracts, revenue, retention, and customer satisfaction.
- Internal benchmark data and failure cases.
- IP transfer/license agreements.
- Non-public advisor agreements and time commitments.
